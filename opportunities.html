Area of Opportunity



Possible Improvements

While our livestream was a success, there are several areas where improvements could be made to enhance future broadcasts. One significant opportunity lies in better organization and structure. Our stream had moments of spontaneity, which made it fun and dynamic, but at times, the lack of a clear plan resulted in occasional confusion. Establishing a more structured flow, such as a set game order, predefined discussion points, and smoother transitions between activities, could help maintain viewer interest and reduce downtime. A schedule displayed on the screen could also help viewers know what to expect next, keeping them engaged for longer.

Another key area for improvement is audience interaction. While we did engage with chat conversations, there are additional strategies we could implement to deepen viewer involvement. Features like live polls, interactive challenges, or even small giveaways could encourage more active participation. Additionally, setting up dedicated segments where viewers choose the next game or suggest in-game challenges could increase audience engagement. Using tools like Twitch extensions or chatbots to enhance interactivity could also make the experience more immersive.

Technical aspects are another crucial area for improvement. Our streamâ€™s audio and video quality could be enhanced to provide a smoother experience for viewers. Ensuring that microphones are clear and background noise is minimized would improve communication between stream participants. Similarly, optimizing stream settings for stable video quality and reducing lag would create a more professional and enjoyable viewing experience. Investing in an overlay design for the stream could also add a more polished and visually appealing look.

Lastly, promotion and outreach could help expand our audience. While our stream was primarily attended by classmates and friends, we could attract a broader audience by sharing the stream on social media platforms and Discord communities. Creating short highlight clips and posting them on TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube could also help draw in new viewers.
